Post-Civil War Incarceration

Data Justice & The Legacy of the Convict Leasing System

In the era following the American Civil War, the Southern legal landscape underwent a radical transformation. While the 13th Amendment technically abolished slavery, its "punishment for crime" loophole allowed for a new era of state-sanctioned labor exploitation known as the Convict Leasing System.

As a Data Analyst focusing on Computational Social Science, my research examines how the "Black Codes" of the 1860s and 70s created a statistical pipeline that disproportionately targeted formerly enslaved individuals to fuel the industrialization of the New South.

About Quiana
